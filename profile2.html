Meet Susan Wojcicki

​
Susan Wojcicki
​ ​

Background

​

Sarah showcases that you can grow from being in a small business to being a part of the most successful media platforms in the world! In 1998, Google cofounders Sergey Brin and Larry Page rented Wojcicki's garage in Menlo Park, California and developed Google's search engine there. In 2006, she advocated for the $1.65 billion acquisition of YouTube, which she has run since 2014. Before Google, Wojciki worked in the marketing department at Intel and as a management consultant at Bain & Company.​


​ ​

Why Is She Known? Impacts/Works

​ ​

She was hired in 1999 as Google employee number 16, and worked on everything from AdSense and Google Analytics to Google Books and Google Images. Susan Wojcicki is CEO of Alphabet subsidiary YouTube, which has 2 billion monthly users.
